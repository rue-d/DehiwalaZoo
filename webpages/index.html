WELCOME TO THE WILD SIDE

The Dehiwala National Zoo is one of Asia's oldest zoos, with a magnificent array of exotic and indigenous animals. Dehiwala Zoo now holds 72 mammal species, 65 bird species, 31 reptile species, 89 fish species, three amphibian families, and 30 butterfly species, among others. Due to breeding and free-living bird colonies, the total number of animals ranges from 2500 to 3000. Dehiwala Zoo is well-known among visitors not only for its variety of indigenous and exotic species, but also for its beautiful greenery landscaping and rare plant-life, Through good animal welfare, the Zoo supports and promotes conservation by reproducing rare and endangered species, while giving them the care and support they need

NEWS

relocated injured elephants now back in the reserve

On the 14th of December 2021, 5 elephants were found shot by poachers close to the Pinnawala Elephant Reserve, Our Rescue Team was called onsite to tend to these Giants, whilst our transportation department were getting ready to move them to our Vet unit. All 5 of the elephants were in a critical condition and were clutching to their dear lives.


Today, these giants are doing well, thanks to our medical team. The elephants are moved into our reserve, which will be open to the public hopefully after the curfew is lifted.
